What Is Indian Masala Chai?

Anyone who at least once tries this amazing tea flavored with a bouquet of spices will forever remember its delicious taste, unsurpassed aroma, and amazing invigorating effect. The name “masala” is translated as “a mixture of spices”.

The drink is mainly prepared on the basis of Assam black tea. This component got into the recipe after the British founded a tea plantation in Assam.

For the first time, the benefits of Indian masala tea with the addition of milk and spices were appreciated by slaves. The miraculous liquid not only effectively quenched thirst, but also gave a surge of vitality, and energy, and contributed to an increase in working capacity and endurance.

Masala Chai Ingredients

The classic masala chai recipe consists of five main ingredients:

  • Black leaf tea
  • Milk
  • Spices
  • Sweetener
  • Drinking water
But you can let your imagination run wild and create your own variation!

Tea

Preference should be given to large-leaf Assam black tea known for its rich taste and spicy-floral aroma. However, there are many variations — even green tea-based versions like Kahwa in Kashmir.

Milk

Originally made with buffalo milk, masala chai is now widely prepared with cow's milk or alternatives like almond, coconut, or soy milk. Some even use condensed milk or ice cream for exotic versions.

Spices

A classic mix includes green cardamom, black pepper, star anise, cinnamon sticks, and cloves. Ginger and cardamom are essential, while exotic options range from saffron and rose petals to bay leaves and pine nuts.
